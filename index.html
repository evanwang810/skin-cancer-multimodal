Where the model sees benign and malignant

Each point is one dermoscopy lesion the model never saw during training, placed by the features it learned. Hover for a preview, click for the photo, the Grad-CAM, and the prediction.

How the model works

Two encoders read the case in parallel, their features are fused, and one layer turns that into a probability.

1
Image encoder
A ResNet50 pretrained on ImageNet, fine-tuned on dermoscopy, maps the 224×224 photo to a feature vector.
+
2
Metadata encoder
A small network reads age, sex, and lesion location into its own feature vector.
→
3
Fuse
The two vectors are concatenated into one combined representation of the lesion.
→
4
Classify
A final layer outputs P(malignant); a threshold tuned on validation turns it into a label.

The scatter is that fused vector reduced to two dimensions with PCA, so lesions the model reads as similar sit close together and the benign and malignant groups tend to pull apart. The axes carry no clinical unit on their own, they are just the two directions of largest variation in what the model learned. A clean split is a good sign; cases mixed in the middle are where the model is least certain.
